What is a soul tie?

When two people come together sexually, a spiritual bond forms. God designed this specifically for covenant marriage — so that husband and wife become one flesh. Genesis 2:24 says "the two shall become one flesh" — this is not just a metaphor, it is a spiritual reality.

A soul tie is a spiritual connection between two people that persists beyond the physical encounter. It can be formed through:

Sexual intercourse (inside or outside of marriage)
Deep emotional dependency on another person
Exposure to pornographic images — ties can form even with images
Abusive relationships — abuse creates ungodly ties through trauma bonding
Occult rituals that involve sexual elements

Ungodly soul ties give the enemy legal access to your mind, emotions, and body through that person — their wounds, their demons, their sin patterns can influence you even years after the relationship ended.
Why do soul ties matter?

Soul ties explain things that pure psychology cannot:

— Why you cannot stop thinking about someone even though you want to
— Why you repeat the same relationship patterns with different people
— Why past partners seem to still have power over your emotions
— Why you feel empty after sexual encounters outside marriage
— Why sexual sin seems to create a growing appetite rather than satisfaction

"Do you not know that he who unites himself with a prostitute is one with her in body? For it is said, 'The two will become one flesh.'" — 1 Corinthians 6:16

The intimacy with God connection:

Soul ties also block intimacy with God. The connection that was designed to be given to God — and within covenant to a spouse — has been redirected to other people and images. This is why people with many ungodly soul ties often feel spiritually dry, unable to pray, and distant from God. Breaking them restores that connection.
How do you break a soul tie?

Step 1 — Acknowledge
Name the person or image specifically before God. Do not be vague. Specificity is important in the spiritual realm. God already knows — this is about your conscious agreement with truth.

Step 2 — Repent
Repentance means turning — not just feeling sorry, but choosing a different direction. If the sin was done to you (abuse), you still repent of any agreement you made with the lie it created about you.

Step 3 — Renounce
Verbally renounce the soul tie. Spoken words have spiritual power — this is why demons use words to bind and God's Word sets free.

Step 4 — Command
Command every spirit that entered through this tie to leave — in the name of Jesus and by His blood.

Step 5 — Fill the space
Invite the Holy Spirit to fill what was emptied. A cleaned house must be filled — Matthew 12:44-45.
"Flee from sexual immorality. Every other sin a person commits is outside the body, but the sexually immoral person sins against his own body." — 1 Corinthians 6:18
Before you begin
Come before God now. Ask the Holy Spirit to bring to mind every ungodly soul tie you need to break — He will show you. You may be surprised what He reveals. There is no condemnation in this process — only cleansing. The blood of Jesus covers every sin you confess.
Declaration 1 — Acknowledgement and repentance
"Father God, I come to You through the blood of Jesus. I acknowledge before You the ungodly soul ties I have formed. I name them now: [name each person/image specifically in your mind]. I repent of my involvement in each of these. Where I was sinned against, I repent of any agreement I made with the lies those experiences created. I ask for Your complete forgiveness through the blood of Jesus."
📖 1 John 1:9 — "If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive and cleanse us"
📖 Psalm 51:10 — "Create in me a clean heart, O God"
Declaration 2 — Renouncing and commanding
"I now renounce every ungodly soul tie I have named before God. I break every spiritual bond formed outside of covenant marriage. I sever these connections now under the blood of Jesus. I command every spirit that entered my life through these ties to leave me now in the name of Jesus Christ. You have no legal right to remain. The blood of Jesus has cancelled your claim. Go to where Jesus sends you. Leave now."
📖 Matthew 18:18 — "Whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven"
📖 Colossians 2:14-15 — "He cancelled the charge of our legal indebtedness... triumphing over them by the cross"
Declaration 3 — Filling and sealing
"Holy Spirit, I now invite You to fill every place that has been emptied. Fill my mind, my emotions, my body, and my spirit with Your presence. I seal this work with the blood of Jesus. I declare that I belong completely to God — spirit, soul, and body. No ungodly tie has any further claim on me. I am free. I am whole. I am God's."
📖 1 Corinthians 6:19-20 — "You are not your own; you were bought at a price"
📖 Romans 8:11 — "The Spirit who raised Jesus from the dead is living in you"
Practical steps — what to do now
1
Remove all connections. Delete messages, photos, and contact details from past partners. Remove objects, gifts, or anything that keeps the soul tie active. This is not bitterness — it is wisdom.
2
Stop all contact for 40 days minimum. This includes social media watching. Every time you check their profile, you reopen the connection. 40 days is a biblical number of separation and transformation.
3
When memories return, redirect — don't fight. Fighting images strengthens them. Instead, say: "I cover this memory in the blood of Jesus" and immediately redirect your mind to a Scripture, a worship song, or an act of service.
4
Forgive the person. Forgiveness does not mean what happened was acceptable. It means you release them from your judgment and give that judgment to God. Unforgiveness keeps the soul tie active.
5
Take communion. Communion applies the blood of Jesus directly to this healing. Do it specifically in the context of breaking these ties — it is a powerful spiritual act of covenant with God rather than with past partners.
6
For pornography soul ties — these require additional renouncing because images create neural pathways as well as spiritual ties. The mind module will help you renew the spirit of your mind in this specific area.
If you were abused

Soul ties formed through abuse are not your fault. But they are still real and still need to be broken. The declaration is slightly different: you do not repent of the abuse done to you — you repent of any agreement with the lies it created (that you are worthless, that you deserved it, that God was not there). You then break the tie and command every spirit that entered through that abuse to leave.

If you have experienced severe abuse, please do not do this alone. Seek a trained deliverance minister who can walk through this with you.

If you feel you need more help, a trained deliverance minister can walk through this with you. This work is more effective with support. You do not have to do this alone.
