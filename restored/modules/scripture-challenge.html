Module · Scripture vs Christian Culture
Power, not religion
Christian culture often gives behaviour modification without power. Scripture gives resurrection life through the Holy Spirit. This module challenges the religious mindset and calls you to the real thing.
What is the difference between Christian culture and Scripture?

Christian culture says: "Try harder. Pray more. Read your Bible. Stop sinning." These are hollow instructions without the power of the Holy Spirit.

Scripture says: "The same power that raised Christ from the dead lives in you." (Romans 8:11). "You will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes on you." (Acts 1:8). "I can do all things through Christ who gives me strength." (Philippians 4:13).

Christian culture often produces guilt and performance. Scripture produces transformation and freedom.

"Having a form of godliness but denying its power — have nothing to do with such people." — 2 Timothy 3:5
Why does this matter for sexual healing?

Christian culture around sexuality typically offers:
— "Just don't do it" (behaviour modification without power)
— Shame and condemnation (which drives people away from God and deeper into sin)
— Silence (sex is not discussed at all, leaving people completely unprepared)
— Performance standards (purity culture that crushes rather than heals)

Scripture offers:
— The Holy Spirit's actual transforming power
— No condemnation for those in Christ Jesus
— Honest engagement with sexuality as God designed it
— The cross as the gateway to freedom — not as punishment but as the door to the promised land

The cross is the gateway — not the destination. Denying yourself and taking up your cross daily leads not to misery but to resurrection life, the promised land, and the fullness of God.

"For the joy set before Him, He endured the cross, scorning its shame." — Hebrews 12:2
How do you shift from religion to relationship?

1. Stop trying to manage sin — start pursuing God's presence
The presence of God is more powerful than any addiction. "Those who live according to the Spirit have their minds set on what the Spirit desires." (Romans 8:5). Fill yourself with God — the sin loses its grip.

2. Replace rules with revelation
Rules say "don't do this." Revelation says "you don't want to do this because you have tasted something better." Ask the Holy Spirit for revelation, not just information.

3. Understand the cross properly
Jesus despised the shame of the cross but endured it for the joy ahead. The cross is painful — it is dying to yourself, your desires, your kingdom. But on the other side is resurrection. The promised land. Your true self in God.

4. Walk by the Spirit, not by the law
"If you are led by the Spirit, you are not under the law." (Galatians 5:18). The Holy Spirit guides, convicts, empowers, and transforms. Religion demands performance. The Spirit produces fruit.
Culture vs Scripture — key challenges
1
Culture: "Purity means avoiding sex." Scripture: Purity means a clean heart before God — which for married couples means the marriage bed is holy and undefiled. Hebrews 13:4
2
Culture: "God is disappointed when you fail." Scripture: "There is no condemnation for those in Christ Jesus." God is running toward you, not away. Romans 8:1, Luke 15:20
3
Culture: "Sex is shameful and not to be discussed." Scripture: God invented sex and called it good. Song of Solomon is explicitly erotic. Shame around sex is not biblical — it came from the fall. Genesis 2:25
4
Culture: "Just pray harder and it will go away." Scripture: Freedom requires repentance, renouncing, deliverance, and the filling of the Holy Spirit. Some things only come out through prayer and fasting. Matthew 17:21
5
Culture: "Christians cannot be demonised." Scripture: Christians can and do experience demonic oppression. Paul describes spiritual warfare as ongoing (Ephesians 6:10-18). Ignorance does not protect — knowledge and authority do.
Declaration — choosing power over religion
"I renounce the spirit of religion that offers form without power. I renounce guilt-driven performance and shame-based Christianity. I choose the real gospel — the power of the Holy Spirit, the blood of Jesus, and the resurrection life that is available to me now. I am not trying harder — I am pressing into God. His presence is my freedom. His cross is my gateway to the promised land. I choose life."
