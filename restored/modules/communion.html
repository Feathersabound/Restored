Module · Communion and the Blood
Breaking bread together
Communion is not a ritual. It is covenant renewal, spiritual protection, and actively applying the blood of Jesus to your life, your marriage, and your home.
What is communion?

Communion — breaking bread and sharing the cup — is a covenant act. It declares the body and blood of Jesus Christ applied to your life. It is remembrance, but far more than remembrance. It is a spiritual transaction — applying what Jesus paid on the cross to your present reality.

The blood of Jesus is the legal basis for everything — forgiveness, deliverance, healing, and the cancellation of every demonic legal right. When you take communion, you are actively applying that blood.

"Whenever you eat this bread and drink this cup, you proclaim the Lord's death until He comes." — 1 Corinthians 11:26
Why is communion essential for healing?

The blood of Jesus is the answer to every legal claim the enemy makes against you. When demons accuse you, the blood answers. When your past condemns you, the blood answers. When generational curses seek to operate, the blood answers.

For married couples, taking communion together is one of the most powerful spiritual acts available. It:

— Renews the marriage covenant before God
— Invites the presence of Christ into the marriage
— Creates a wall of spiritual protection around the home
— Declares that Christ is the third strand of the cord
— Breaks the power of arguments, division, and Leviathan

For singles, taking communion regularly creates a spiritual covering and keeps the blood actively applied to your healing journey.

"Let us approach the throne of grace with confidence through the blood." — Hebrews 4:16
How do you take communion?

Use bread (any bread) and juice or wine. This does not need to be in a church — God honours communion taken at home in faith.

1. Come with reverence — this is a holy act
2. Examine your heart — repent of any known sin first (1 Corinthians 11:28)
3. Declare over the bread: "By His body broken for me, I receive healing and wholeness."
4. Declare over the cup: "By His blood shed for me, every sin is forgiven, every legal claim cancelled, every curse broken."
5. Receive in faith — believe that what you are declaring is spiritually real and active

Married couples should do this together regularly — weekly if possible. Not just at church — in the home, at the dinner table, as a covenant act before God.
Communion declaration — the bread
"Lord Jesus, I take this bread as a declaration that by Your body broken for me, I receive healing — physical, emotional, sexual, and spiritual. Every wound in my body, every trauma in my soul, every broken place in my sexuality — I bring it to the cross. By Your stripes I am healed."
📖 Isaiah 53:5 — "By His wounds we are healed"
📖 Matthew 26:26 — "This is my body, broken for you"
Communion declaration — the cup
"Lord Jesus, I take this cup as a declaration that by Your blood shed for me, every sin is forgiven and cleansed. Every soul tie is severed. Every generational curse is broken. Every legal right the enemy has had in my life is cancelled. I am covered. I am clean. I am in covenant with God. The blood speaks for me."
📖 Matthew 26:28 — "This is my blood of the covenant, poured out for the forgiveness of sins"
📖 Revelation 12:11 — "They overcame him by the blood of the Lamb"
For married couples — covenant declaration
"Lord Jesus, we take communion together as husband and wife. We renew our covenant with You and with each other. We declare that You are the third strand of our cord — and a cord of three strands is not easily broken. We cover our marriage in Your blood. We cover our home, our children, and our intimacy in Your blood. What You have joined together, no enemy shall separate."
📖 Ecclesiastes 4:12 — "A cord of three strands is not quickly broken"
📖 Matthew 19:6 — "What God has joined together, let no one separate"
The blood speaks
Hebrews 12:24 says the blood of Jesus "speaks a better word" than the blood of Abel. Abel's blood cried out for justice and judgment. Jesus's blood speaks mercy, forgiveness, and freedom. Every time you take communion, that blood speaks on your behalf in the heavenly courts — cancelling every accusation, closing every legal claim, and declaring you clean.
